Where We Gather

Tate House

2973 GA-53 · Tate, Georgia 30177

A mansion that never felt imported or imposed — like it had simply risen out of the Georgia hills on its own.

Colonel Sam Tate built this house in 1926 from the pink marble of his own quarries — the same stone used in the Lincoln Memorial and the U.S. Supreme Court. Pink Georgia marble reads less as pink than as warm: nearly ivory in morning light, deepening to a burnished rose by afternoon.

The grounds spread across 27 acres framed by the Appalachian foothills, with English gardens terracing down from the house and a vine-draped conservatory connecting the private quarters to the formal rooms.
